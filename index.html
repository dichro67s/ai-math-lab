About

Research at the intersection of AI and mathematics

SNU AI Mathematics Lab conducts research on how machines can formalize, solve, generate, and verify mathematical knowledge.

Lab overview

We study mathematical reasoning with AI, with particular attention to formalization, evaluation, and verification. Our work includes autoformalization, benchmark design, public evaluation through leaderboards, automated problem solving, automated problem generation, theorem generation, and AI mathematics verification.

Main research directions

Each topic can later link to a project page, benchmark page, dataset, or repository.

01

Autoformalization

Transforming informal mathematics into formal representations suitable for proof assistants and automated reasoning systems.

02

Benchmarks & Leaderboards

Designing rigorous evaluation suites and public leaderboards for autoformalization and AI mathematics systems.

03

Automated Problem Solving

Developing systems that solve mathematical problems reliably across formal and informal settings.

04

Automated Problem Generation

Creating high-quality mathematical problems for training, evaluation, and curriculum design.

05

Theorem Generation

Exploring models that propose meaningful conjectures, lemmas, and theorems in mathematically useful forms.

06

AI Mathematics Verification

Building verification pipelines that check correctness, consistency, and formal validity of mathematical outputs.
